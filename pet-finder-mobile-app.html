Petfinder

App Design

Find your pet no matter where and how far away it is.

Pet owners go through unimaginable worries when their pet is lost. The goal was to reduce that fear with a transparent and informative app.
Areas
Research, Ideation, Visual Design, UI,UX.
Duration
4 weeks

Highlights

Goal
Develop an efficient tool for tracking and locating pets, that provides a positive and comforting experience for both owners and pets.
User problem
The research found that products, both physical and digital, are not very intuitive, which generates problems of use with users with disabilities.
User problem
The size and weight of the physical products are not adaptable for all types of pets, making it difficult for small pets to use them.
Main challenge
How might we design a product that is accessible and easy to handle for users with physical and visual disabilities.

Knowing the problems

To find the problems and needs of the users, desk research was conducted followed by user interviews , this data was converted into an empathy map which was then translated into a table of "needs "and "actions " . At the end, apersona and a user journey map were created.
The empathy map was made to synthesize everything documented by the users.
A "Needs "and "Actions "table was created to understand these behaviors in depth.
The user 's profile was captured in a persona.
A user journey was created to understand the different touchpoints and opportunities for improvement.
Final insights...
At the end of this phase, some insights were gained on what the app experience should be like.
Accesible
Many users have pets for help or companionship, so designing for them is important.
Informative
Always keep in mind to report the pet 's condition over other things that may be visually appealing.
Intuitive
Many users may be distant from technology, so the design has to be intuitive.

Generating ideas.

After understanding the problems, aProblem Statement was drawn up and ideas were generated to solve the problem.
Problem statement
Alicia is a 34 year old lawyer who has lost one of her pets in the past and is looking for an effective and simple way to keep an eye on her pet. This way she can feel safe and calm when she is not at her pet 's side.
Priority Matrix
The criteria was based on project time, designing only the ideas that could make the cut.

An accessible structure.

After deciding which ideas would be designed, organizing the content so that the user could have a good understanding of it was crucial.
Task flows
Creation of Task Flows for the main user actions.
Sitemap
The development of a Sitemap served to organize the content of the app.
Your pet 's safety in just a few clicks

Main features

With the help of user and market research, I added some important features to the application to make it more effective , have higher conversion and user satisfaction .
